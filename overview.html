Advanced Theory of Agricultural Machinery

A 15-week interactive course syllabus

Course Overview

Course Description

Tractor and implement, analysis on hitching systems and operating. Stability and dynamics behavior of tractor and implement, dynamics of tire, steering, drifting, slipping, sideways and rearward overturning of tractor, tractor efficiency improvement, guidance systems for tractors.

Key Information

  • Duration: 15 Weeks
  • Total Hours: 45 Hours (3 hours/week)
  • Focus: Advanced Dynamics, Stability, and Systems

Syllabus Explorer

The course is divided into five key modules. Select a module to see the weekly topics and explore the course's progression.

Module 1: Foundations & Hitching Systems

Week 1: Introduction to Advanced Tractor-Implement Systems

Course overview, review of tractor systems, classification of implements, and principles of advanced dynamic analysis.

Week 2: Analysis of Hitching Systems I

Kinematics and force analysis of 3-point hitch systems (mounted, semi-mounted). Drawbar dynamics.

Week 3: Implement Operation & Control

Dynamics of implement operation. Principles of draft, position, and float control. Hydraulic system response.

Module 2: Tire, Soil & Steering Dynamics

Week 4: Tire Mechanics & Terramechanics

Introduction to terramechanics. Tire construction and properties. Modeling tire-soil interaction.

Week 5: Traction, Slipping, and Rolling Resistance

Analysis of traction models. Factors affecting slip. Calculation of rolling resistance. Tractive efficiency.

Week 6: Steering Dynamics & Drifting

Ackermann steering geometry. High-speed steering analysis. Vehicle handling characteristics. Causes and analysis of drifting.

Module 3: Tractor Stability & Safety

Week 7: Static & Dynamic Stability

Determining Center of Gravity (CG). Static stability analysis. Stability polygon. Introduction to dynamic stability.

Week 8: Rearward Overturning Analysis

Static and dynamic conditions for rearward overturning. Effects of hitch point, drawbar load, and acceleration.

Week 9: Sideways Overturning Analysis

Static and dynamic conditions for sideways overturning. Analysis on slopes and during turns (centrifugal force). ROPS design principles.

Module 4: Performance & Advanced Systems

Week 10: Tractor Efficiency Improvement

Overall tractor efficiency. Engine performance, transmission matching, and power take-off (PTO) dynamics. Ballasting strategies.

Week 11: Dynamics of Tractor-Implement Combinations

Coupled motion analysis. Dynamic behavior of tractor with large implements. Handling and stability of combined systems.

Week 12: Introduction to Guidance Systems

Principles of precision agriculture. GPS, RTK, and GNSS systems. Sensor technologies (vision, laser, ultrasonic).

Week 13: Autonomous Tractor Technology

Autosteer systems. Path planning algorithms (A* , B-spline). Vehicle control systems and ISOBUS communication.

Module 5: Synthesis & Review

Week 14: Case Studies & Future Trends

Analysis of real-world case studies in machinery dynamics and failure. Future trends in agricultural robotics and automation.

Week 15: Course Review & Final Examination

Comprehensive review of all course topics. Final examination covering theory, analysis, and application.
